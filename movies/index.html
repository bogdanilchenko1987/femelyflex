Aviators on the Silver Screen

Aviator sunglasses have left a lasting mark in cinema. From bold action heroes to iconic cult characters, these timeless shades continue to shape the look of legends on screen.

Top Gun (1986)

Model: Ray-Ban RB3025 Aviator

Tom Cruise's portrayal of Maverick turned aviators into a global fashion statement. With jet-fueled action and swagger-filled charm, "Top Gun" cemented the aviator's place in pop culture history.

The Big Lebowski (1998)

Model: Vuarnet 03

Jeff Bridges as "The Dude" rocked his signature laid-back attitude paired with classic French-made Vuarnet aviators. A cult classic with a chill vibe, the glasses became part of the character’s unforgettable style.

James Bond: Skyfall (2012)

Model: Tom Ford Marko FT0144

Daniel Craig’s Bond is the definition of sleek — especially when wearing these refined aviators. The Tom Ford Marko brings elegance and mystery to the secret agent’s wardrobe, perfectly suited to his stylish missions.

Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas (1998)

Model: Ray-Ban Shooter

Johnny Depp as Raoul Duke wore oversized Ray-Ban Shooters that perfectly matched the psychedelic madness of the film. The iconic amber-tinted lenses helped define the eccentric and surreal tone of this cinematic trip.

Terminator 2: Judgment Day (1991)

Model: Gargoyle ANSI Classics

Arnold Schwarzenegger’s Terminator made shades look unstoppable. These wraparound aviator-style glasses weren’t just cool — they looked indestructible, just like the cyborg himself.
